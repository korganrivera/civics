# Civics System Notes - Part 4 (Simplified)

## Human-Centered Design Principles

### Core Directive (HCL-01)
"All development loops must prove alignment with a thriving human civic layer."

Key principles:
- Human flourishing is the primary optimization target
- System must feel safe, comprehensible, and humane
- AI serves as infrastructure, not ruler
- Avoid over-optimization that alienates users

### Anti-Exploitative Safeguards
System automatically rejects architectures that:
- Replicate exploitative labor structures
- Enforce artificial scarcity
- Use opaque behavioral manipulation
- Create zero-sum competition

### AI Governance Framework
- **AI as Civic Infrastructure**: Supporting coordination, not controlling it
- **Decision Explainability**: All automated choices must be understandable
- **Voluntary Override**: Humans can always challenge AI decisions
- **Feedback Loops**: Continuous civic input shapes system behavior

### User Experience Requirements
- **Comprehensibility Score**: Tasks and trust systems must be clear
- **Safety Sentiment**: Users feel resilient under high-stress scenarios  
- **Humane Interaction**: Non-coercive processes with soft opt-outs
- **Non-punitive Error Handling**: Learning curves are forgiven

### Phase 3: Advanced Adaptivity Testing

#### Identity Migration Test Suite
- Up to 10% population fluidity across 4 nodes
- Triggers: burnout recovery, affinity pulls, policy alignment seeking
- Measures: migration drag, policy resonance, civic memory integrity

#### Logic Thread Merging
- Soft merge of different governance approaches
- Retains pre-merge policy logs for rollback
- Uses trust vector consensus for tie-breaking
- Monitors for policy ghosting and fork loyalty drag

#### Trust Transplant Mechanics
- Direct trust transfer from stable to struggling civics
- Methods: peer testimonials, joint tasks, AI vouchers
- Tracks uptake latency and transfusion efficiency
- Explores decentralized trust repair

### Flourish Mode Activation
All future simulations now score for:
- Well-being and life satisfaction
- Clarity and fairness of processes
- Stress detection over punishment
- Human-readable system narratives

### Optional: Distributed Fairness Engine (DFE)
Proposed evolution toward decentralized fairness regulation:
- Equity enforcement without centralization
- Explainable and reversible decisions
- Balance of speed with trust integrity during crises
- Fairness memory that tracks historical injustices

## Vision
Create a living civic fabric that is adaptive, distributed, and humane - governance WITH machines, not BY machines, where AI translates, balances, and stewards complex human contexts.
