### Core Idea

Traditional systems rely on outside enforcement (laws, bosses, money pressure).
Civics relies on internal tracking, feedback, and repair.

Humans naturally create inefficient, coercive systems because we can’t track all needs, efforts, and outcomes at once.
The AI system (“Auren”) fills this gap by holding data across time, people, and resources.

Auren doesn’t replace human judgment.
It supports it by:

• Tracking contributions and needs
• Detecting imbalance, fraud, and drift
• Coordinating work and resources in real time

This allows a fair, regenerative system to function long-term.

Without AI support, Civic systems collapse under untracked obligations and inefficiencies.

---

## Civics System Overview

**Technical name:** Participatory Symbiotic Economy
**Common name:** Civics
**Members:** Civics
**Purpose:**
Coordinate production and exchange of goods, services, and labor through transparent tracking, contribution-based value, and decentralized trust.

---

### Core Principles

• Access is based on contribution, not profit or accumulation
• Value is logged openly and verified
• Participation is flexible and voluntary
• Trust is earned over time
• The system works even without AI or power (fallback modes)

---

### Infrastructure

#### AI System (Auren)

Functions:

• Logs contributions and resource changes
• Assigns tasks based on need, skill, availability, and trust
• Flags anomalies and fraud
• Mediates simple disputes
• Works via voice, text, mobile
• Runs in reduced/offline modes if needed

---

#### Communication

Primary: headset or mobile app
Backup: in-person logging, written records, peer reports
AI is present in all transactions

---

#### Contribution Tracking

Methods:

• Task logging (manual or voice)
• Delivery confirmation
• Peer verification

Value weighting:

• Effort (time, skill)
• Usefulness (need, scarcity)
• Reliability (quality, consistency)

Privacy:

• Sensitive tasks can be anonymized
• Contributions still count abstractly

---

### Roles & Trust

Levels:

• Observer – passive learner/user
• Contributor – active participant
• Steward – trusted mediator/manager
• System Committee – temporary human group activated by AI for major decisions

Progression based on:

• Verified history
• Consistency
• Peer support

Anti-gaming safeguards:

• Cross-group endorsements
• AI detects collusion loops
• Influence mapping (“trust shadows”)
• Random audits of trust patterns

### Examples

**Soap production**

• Civic makes soap

• Flags ingredient needs
• Routes distribution

**Truck crash losing tomatoes**

• Error logged

---

### Ethics & Limits

• No profit motive
• Surplus flows back to community
• Manipulation and deception penalized
• All exchanges logged (with privacy options)

---

### System Extensions

• Works with 1–2 people or large groups
• Off-grid paper fallback
• Groups can merge by syncing logs and trust data

---

## Dispute Resolution

1. Peer mediation (AI assists with summaries)
2. Conflict Council chosen randomly from trusted Stewards
3. Transparent logs (optional anonymity)

Anonymized case summaries are stored to help AI suggest precedents and prevent repeat conflicts.

---

## AI Federation & Resilience

• Each community runs a local AI node
• Nodes sync asynchronously (no single failure point)
• Sync friction tracking warns when nodes drift too far apart
• Quorum mode: multiple nodes simulate AI functions if one fails
• Pre-cached heuristics handle emergencies

Heuristic relevance decays over time, slower during disasters.

---

## External Economy Interface

• Civics can interact with money systems through “bridge zones”
• All inflows go through a buffer ledger before entering communal pool

Value conversion uses dynamic formulas:

adjusted value =
effort × scarcity × relevance × urgency

(no fixed exchange rates)

Once contributed, resources become communal (no private ownership).

---

## Data Governance

• AI base is open-source
• Tiered log access:

– Personal logs: user + system
– Aggregates: Stewards/Committees
– Full logs: Trust Court only

• Nodes exchange signed data digests when syncing or merging

Uses Merkle Forest / DAG-style ledgers (not energy-heavy blockchains) for auditability.

---

## Burnout & Participation Health

• No mandatory minimum contributions
• Status fades naturally but is easy to regain
• Contribution banking allows rest after high effort
• Civic Support Roles assist people in crisis

New low-energy role:

**Observational Validator** – passive participation, anomaly flagging

Latent contribution scores track long-term capability without penalty.

## Trust as Infrastructure

Trust works like Git:

• Branches for experimental policies or subgroups
• Merges with conflict resolution
• Forks allow local variation

Trust affects routing, access, and coordination.
