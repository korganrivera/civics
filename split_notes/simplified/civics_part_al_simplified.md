## User Experience Examples

### Day in the Life: Civic Member Perspective

#### Morning Resource Coordination
- System shows available tasks matching your skills and energy
- Explains why you're suggested: "Your coordination style + current energy level"  
- Provides context about community need and alternatives
- No pressure - genuine choice in participation

#### Decision Participation
- Voting on community policy (e.g., energy usage)
- System weights vote based on relevant experience and past engagement
- Option to defer: "Others closer to this issue have higher stake - you may abstain with no penalty"
- Thoughtful deferral recognized, not penalized

#### Conflict Resolution
- Previous proposal was auto-deprioritized by trust system
- Full explanation provided: "Here's what happened and your options for appeal"
- Transparent algorithmic decision-making
- Clear path to challenge or modify outcomes

#### Emotional Support Integration
- System detects repetitive viewing of same decision page
- Gentle prompt: "Noticing some hesitation - want peer perspective or just need space?"
- Connection to someone with similar decision patterns
- No pressure approach, successful emotional assistance logged

## Implementation Realities

### Hard Realities for Early Groups
- **Heavy Workload**: Core tasks (growing, cooking, building, maintaining) are physically demanding
- **Capacity Limits**: Not everyone can contribute equally due to health, time, or skill constraints
- **Resource Scarcity**: Groups start with limited tools, space, and financial resources
- **External Pressure**: Operating within capitalist legal and economic framework

### Capacity Matching Systems
- **Partial Participation**: Ways for people to contribute within their actual limits
- **Shadow Tasks**: Invisible support work that helps community without formal recognition
- **Energy Tracking**: AI helps match tasks to actual human capacity, not ideal capacity
- **Burnout Prevention**: System designed to sustain long-term rather than optimize short-term output

### Bootstrap Approach
Groups build what they can support piece by piece rather than implementing full system at once. Focus on proving core mechanics (trade, decision-making, conflict resolution) work before adding sophisticated features.
