A distributed governance system where:
- People organize into small "civics" (communities)
- AI assists with coordination and trust tracking
- Trust is quantified and impacts decision-making power
- Resources flow based on contribution and need

- Trust tracked between individuals and communities
- Trust affects voting weight and resource access
- Trust can decay over time or be rebuilt
- System forgives honest mistakes vs punishes malicious behavior

1. Local decision-making
2. Cached policies
3. AI consensus (if available)
4. Trust Court (emergency arbitration)

# Civics System Notes - Part 6 (Simplified)

## Power Resistance and Anti-Authoritarian Design

### How the System Prevents Takeover

The civic system is designed to resist authoritarian capture through multiple mechanisms:

#### 1. No Central Point of Control
- No single leader or ruling body
- Power is distributed across nodes and contexts
- Decisions flow through trust networks, not hierarchies

#### 2. Trust Transparency
- All influence is visible and traceable
- Trust scores and relationships are open to community
- Power accumulation triggers automatic scrutiny

#### 3. Adaptive Routing Around Bad Actors
When someone tries to accumulate excessive control:
- **Detection**: System spots influence scaling faster than trust
- **Information Blackouts**: Attempts to hide information are flagged
- **Response**: Not punishment, but automatic rebalancing
  - Visibility amplification to peers
  - Trust transparency reports
  - Slowdown of influence-routing from that node
  - If needed: civic quarantine or redistribution vote

#### 4. Self-Balancing Immune System
Responses are restorative, not punitive:
- Like an immune system recognizing stress
- Routes around problematic actors rather than attacking them
- Maintains system health without violence

#### 5. Narrative and Emotional Transparency
Prevents manipulation through:
- **Confusion**: All decisions are explainable in plain language
- **Fear**: Emotional signals are visible to community
- **Opacity**: Decision processes are traceable and contestable
- Manipulative speech creates tone-trust divergence that lowers influence

### Core Principle: "You Can't Overthrow a System with No Throne"

- Civic power is cooperative, contextual, reversible, and emotionally exposed
- Power can be earned and lost, but cannot be captured
- Influence must be continuously re-earned through trust and contribution

## Crisis Scenario: Total Power Outage

### When AI and Computers Fail

The system is designed for graceful degradation during infrastructure failure:

#### Step 1: Human-Led Protocols
- **Local Physical Protocol Books**: Printed guides for governance without tech
- **Rotating Human Stewards**: Trained in low-tech civic continuity
- **Analog Trust Tokens**: Physical proxies for routing needs and access
- Think "civic CPR" - slow, human, but alive

#### Step 2: Culture Over Code
- People remember trust-building habits
- Conflict resolution defaults to face-to-face methods
- Resource sharing through mutual aid and notice boards
- Elders hold continuity of customs and wisdom

#### Step 3: Trust Adaptation
- Trust doesn't collapse, it slows and thickens
- Decisions become hyper-local and visible
- Community relies on direct perception and proximity
- Still aligned with core principles: fairness, dignity, reciprocity

#### Step 4: Rebuild Protocols
- **Reboot Kits**: EMP-shielded storage of civic essentials
- **Recovery Roles**: Citizens trained in manual system reset
- **Physical Trust Charts**: Help align community memory with last digital records
- **Trust Reflection Sync**: When power returns, blend analog period decisions

### Why It Doesn't Collapse
- People understand the system in non-digital terms
- Logic is visible, empathic, and ritually practiced
- Trust substrate is human-first - AI enhances but never replaces
- System devolves but doesn't dissolve

### Handling Bad Actors During Outages

When power returns and some exploitation occurred during blackout:

#### Memory-Based Recovery
- Community "return ritual" - truth-telling and memory sync
- Subjective accounts treated as signals, not proof
- Pattern detection across multiple testimonies

#### Soft Investigation
- Non-adversarial inquiry circles
- Gentle forensics looking for indirect traces
- Transparency asks rather than accusations

#### Restorative Rebalancing
- Focus on repair rather than punishment
- Resource return and amends-making
- Temporary scope reduction for trust rebuilding
- Rehabilitation over retribution
