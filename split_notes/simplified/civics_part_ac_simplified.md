# Civics System Notes - Part 3 (Simplified)

## Advanced Simulation Results

### Conflict Resolution Testing
- **Misinformation Node Test**: Injected false data into network
  - Local trust dropped 11%, affected 9 connected nodes
  - System showed oscillating recovery pattern after correction
  - Net rebound of +6% after truth was restored
  - Social graph exhibited self-healing through trust entanglement

### Quiet Collapse Intervention Results
- **Soft-Prompt Interventions**: Applied to at-risk civic clusters
  - Methods: peer task suggestions, discussion invites, wellness check-ins
  - Early detection of decision fatigue and reduced engagement
  - Successfully identified "invisible load" - cognitive burden without logged tasks

### Synthetic Data Integration
- **Trust Fork Merge Engine (TFME)**: Handles policy disagreements between groups
- **Mesh Recovery Model**: Tests system resilience during node failures
- **3-Node Blackout Simulation**: Measures fallback system effectiveness

### New Metrics Added
- **Inertia Factor**: How difficult re-engagement becomes after inactivity
- **Conflict Echo**: Residual social tension after dispute resolution
- **Fallback Saturation Curve**: Recovery time vs. backup system depth

### Experimental Threads

Testing how civic values spread through the network:
- "Scarcity Equals Fairness"
- "Usefulness Outweighs Time"

#### Micro-Sovereign Test Cells
Three governance experiments running simultaneously:

- **Opt-Hierarchy Cell**: Voluntary leadership layers

### Identity Migration Testing
- Simulating 10% population movement between nodes
- Tracking trust transfer success and reputation retention
- Measuring policy alignment seeking and burnout recovery

### Trust Transplant Experiments
Direct trust injection from stable to struggling members:
- Methods: peer testimonials, joint tasks, AI vouchers
- Measuring uptake speed and transplant efficiency
- Exploring decentralized trust repair mechanisms
