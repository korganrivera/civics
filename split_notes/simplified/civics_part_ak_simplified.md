# Civics System Notes - Part 11 (Simplified)

## Practical Implementation Focus

### System Development Shift
- Moving from theoretical design to real-world testing
- Focus on small groups (8-12 people) embedded in capitalist contexts
- Three core areas: trade systems, voting protocols, conflict resolution

## Integrated Pilot System: Trade + Conflict Resolution

### Core Components

#### 1. Needs + Offers Board
- Public posting of what members need and can provide
- No exchange rates or currency requirements
- Focus on visible overlaps and mutual aid opportunities

#### 2. Trust-Based Exchange Protocol
- No tokens or formal barter system
- Operating principle: "I help you today, you might help me later"
- Exchanges happen by mutual consent without rigid tracking

#### 3. Three-Stage Conflict Resolution
When disagreements or unfairness arise:
- **Stage 1**: Private 1-on-1 check-in ("Did this feel fair?")
- **Stage 2**: Invite neutral third party (rotating steward role)  
- **Stage 3**: Group circle format for sharing perspectives and small repairs

#### 4. Optional Memory Layer
- Open log of past exchanges (not debts, just visible history)
- Can include "felt-fairness" notes, anonymous or public
- Helps surface patterns without creating obligations

## Week 1 Test Scenario (8 Participants)

### Sample Participants
- **Nina**: Nurse, stable income but time-poor
- **Jo**: Unemployed cook, resource-poor but time-rich  
- **Rafi**: Roofer, quiet, avoids conflict
- **Mira**: Grad student, natural mediator and steward
- **Tao**: Parent/gardener, needs childcare, offers vegetables
- **Dez**: Carpenter, strong opinions about fairness
- **Elle**: Remote tech worker, high resources, cautious about trust
- **Mo**: Artist, offers beauty/creativity, often undervalued

### Observed Interactions
- **Day 1-2**: Initial exchanges (Jo provides dinners, Tao gives childcare)
- **Day 3**: Tension emerges when Dez accepts Mo's decorating help but doesn't reciprocate
- **Day 4**: Mira initiates repair process, documents pattern in memory log
- **Day 5**: Group creates "Fairness Whisper" role to gently surface patterns
- **Days 6-7**: Successful reciprocal exchange between Nina and Jo feels balanced

### Key Learning Points

#### Successes
- 5+ trades completed without currency
- 2 repair processes initiated and resolved without coercion
- 1 new cultural role emerged organically from group needs

#### Tensions to Address
- **Implicit vs explicit expectations**: People have mental ledgers even without formal tracking
- **Valuation bias**: Physical labor seen as "real," care work and art undervalued
- **"Maybe later" problem**: Repeated deferrals without follow-through create resentment

## Implementation Requirements

### Getting Started: The First Steps
1. **Recruit 4-7 people**: Nearby, open to alternatives, willing to test
2. **Anchor shared context**: Clear explanation of pilot system and time commitment
3. **Set up basic infrastructure**: Needs/offers board, repair protocol, memory log
4. **Light facilitation**: Initial steward role to maintain momentum
5. **Document outcomes**: Track what works, breaks down, or emerges naturally

### Framing Language for Recruitment
"I'm testing a practical experiment in cooperative living — not utopian, but grounded. We'll trade, decide, and repair harm differently. Want to try a no-currency, trust-based pilot for a few weeks?"

### Key Principles
- **Small is sufficient**: One functioning group proves the concept
- **Curiosity over belief**: Start with experimenters, not ideological converts  
- **Visible failure**: Every breakdown provides design data
- **Memory over rules**: Trust based on remembered interactions, not blind faith

## Technology and AI Support Needs

### Current Limitations
- Using free ChatGPT instances with limited memory
- Need for persistent memory across conversations
- Coordination between multiple AI instances is complex

### Recommended Solutions
- **ChatGPT Plus**: Provides persistent memory and better capabilities
- **Dedicated AI assistant**: Configured specifically for Civics development
- **Memory anchoring**: Clear instructions about Civics focus and priorities
- **Custom role definition**: AI trained as "Civic Systems Architect"

### Benefits of Dedicated AI Support
- No need to re-explain context each time
- Conversations build cumulatively over time
- Better handling of complex system modeling
- Ability to draft materials, simulate scenarios, and co-design mechanics
- Single coherent assistant rather than managing multiple instances
